\section{Evaluation}

We evaluate our method by comparing the reconstructed XYZ color matching functions from our selected filters to the target XYZ functions, and computing the color difference $\Delta E_{2000}$ under standard reflectance.
We use the Macbeth Color Checker \cite{Pascale2006RGBCO} as our test set, and evaluate under the D65 illuminant.
For each number of filters $N$, we compute the minimum $\Delta E_{2000}$ across all combinations of $N$ filters from the Rosco Supergel set, and plot the relationship between $N$ and $\Delta E_{2000}$.

\begin{center}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{media/deltaE_vs_N_mse.png}
    \captionof{figure}{Best $\Delta E_{2000}$ for each number of filters $N$ }
\end{center}

We also plot the resultant reconstructed XYZ functions for the best filter combination for various results at $N$ filters, and compare it to the target XYZ functions, CIE 1931 in this case.
In the $N=3$ case, we can see visible artifacts in the transmission functions. 
Note that the best MSE solution does not necessarily correspond to the best $\Delta E_{2000}$ solution, and this is most evident at $N=3$ where the 3'rd best MSE achieves the low $\Delta E_{2000}$ of 6.75.

\begin{center}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{media/n3MSETransmission1.png}
    \captionof{figure}{Camera Spectral Sensitivity for best XYZ Reconstruction for $N=3$ }
\end{center}

The best filter combination for $N=3$  achieves a $\Delta E_{2000}$ of 6.7, which is above the just noticeable difference threshold of 2.3, indicating that the reconstructed colors may be distinguishable from the target colors under certain conditions.

However, $N=4$ achieves a $\Delta E_{2000}$ of 1.7, which is below the just noticeable difference threshold, indicating that the reconstructed colors are likely indistinguishable from the target colors under typical viewing conditions.
We demonstrate that this same methodology can be used with CIE 2015 standard observer, with or without NOIR filter. 

\begin{center}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{media/n4MSETransNOIR.png}
    \captionof{figure}{$N=4$, Reconstruction of CIE 2015 Standard Observer using NOIR filter}
\end{center}

Further, $N=10$ achieves a $\Delta E_{2000}$ of 0.08, virtually identical to the target patches. 
Performance is comprable for CIE 2015 or 1931 standard observer. 

\begin{center}

\end{center}

The distribution of $\Delta E_{2000}$ across the 24 Macbeth Color Checker patches for the best filter combination for $N=10$ shows that all patches achieve a $\Delta E_{2000}$ below 0.1.
